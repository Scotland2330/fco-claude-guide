01 · The Basics

What Is Claude — and Why Does It Matter for Your Business?

Claude is an AI assistant made by Anthropic. Think of it as the smartest, fastest member of your team — one who never sleeps, never gets overwhelmed, and can write, analyse, plan, and build alongside you. It is not a search engine. It is not a chatbot. It is a thinking partner that works at the speed of thought.

1

You give it context

Tell Claude about your business, your clients, your tone of voice, your processes. The more it knows about you, the better the output. You can paste in documents, data, emails — anything.

📄 No technical setup needed
2

You give it a task

Write this proposal. Analyse this data. Build me a 90-day plan. Turn these meeting notes into an action list. Draft a response to this client email. Claude handles it.

⚡ Results in seconds, not hours
3

You review and use the output

Claude gives you a first draft that is 80–90% there. You refine it, approve it, send it. You go from blank page to finished work in minutes instead of hours.

✅ You stay in control
💡
The key insight: Most people use Claude like a search engine — they ask it a question and get a generic answer. The founders who get 10× results treat it like a senior colleague: they give it full context, a clear brief, and ask it to think before it acts.

03 · Getting Started

How to Start Using Claude in the Next 30 Minutes

You do not need a developer, a technical background, or any special setup. Here is the exact sequence to go from zero to productive in half an hour.

1

Create your free Claude account

Go to claude.ai and sign up with your email. The free plan is powerful enough to start. Claude Pro (£18/month) unlocks longer conversations and priority access — worth it once you are using it daily.

claude.ai Free to start No credit card needed
2

Create your first Project

In Claude, click Projects in the left sidebar, then New Project. Give it a name like "My Business." This is where you will store all your context — so Claude remembers your business every time you open it.

💡
Why Projects matter: Without a Project, every conversation starts from scratch. With a Project, Claude already knows who you are, what your business does, and how you communicate — before you type a single word.
3

Write your About Me file and upload it

Create a simple text document that tells Claude about your business. Include: your name and role, what your business does, who your clients are, your tone of voice, and your biggest current priorities. Save it as about.md and upload it to your Project.

# About [Your Name] and [Business Name]

## Who I Am
I am [Name], [role] at [Business Name].

## What We Do
[Business Name] helps [target client] to [outcome] by [method].

## Our Clients
Our typical client is [description]. Their main pain points are [list].

## My Tone of Voice
When I communicate, I am [adjectives: e.g. direct, warm, professional].
I avoid [things you don't do: e.g. jargon, corporate speak].

## Current Priorities
Right now I am focused on [top 3 priorities].
Generate my About file automatically →
4

Give Claude its first real task

Now open a new conversation inside your Project and give Claude a real task from your business — not a test, not a demo. Something you actually need done today. The output will be dramatically better than anything you have seen from AI before.

🎯
The golden rule: Always start with "Ask me questions before you start." This forces Claude to clarify before it writes — and the output quality doubles.
5

Add more context files over time

As you use Claude more, build up your context library: a brand voice file, a client profiles file, your key workflows, your pricing and service descriptions. Each file you add makes every future output sharper and more accurate.

about.md brand.md clients.md workflows.md

05 · Build Your Own Agent

How to Build a Claude Agent for Your Business

An "agent" is just Claude with a specific job, a set of instructions, and access to your business context. You do not need to write code to build one. Here is the step-by-step process.

1

Define the agent's job

Pick one specific task your agent will handle. The more specific, the better. Examples: "Proposal Writer," "Client Onboarding Assistant," "Financial Analysis Agent," "SOP Builder."

⚠️
Common mistake: Trying to build one agent that does everything. Build one focused agent first. Master it. Then build the next one.
2

Write the agent's instruction file (CLAUDE.md)

This is the document that tells Claude exactly who it is, what it does, and how it should behave. It is the brain of your agent. Store it in a Claude Project or in a GitHub repository.

# [Agent Name] — [Business Name]

## Role
You are the [role] for [Business Name]. Your job is to [specific task].

## Context
[Business Name] is a [description]. Our clients are [description].
Our tone is [adjectives]. We never [things to avoid].

## How You Work
1. Always ask clarifying questions before starting any task.
2. Use the context files in this Project as your reference.
3. When writing, match the tone in the brand.md file exactly.
4. Always present your output in [format].

## What You Produce
For every task, you will deliver: [list of outputs]

## What You Never Do
- Never make up facts or data
- Never use a tone that does not match our brand
- Never skip the clarifying questions step
3

Build your context library

Upload the files your agent needs to do its job well. At minimum: your about.md, your brand voice file, and any relevant templates or examples. The richer the context, the better the output.

about.md — Who you are brand.md — How you sound clients.md — Who you serve examples/ — Past work samples
Build my context library automatically →
4

Test it with real tasks

Give your agent 5 real tasks from your business. Review the outputs. Refine the instruction file based on what is missing or off. The first version is never perfect — three rounds of refinement is normal.

5

Connect it to your tools (optional but powerful)

Once your agent works well in Claude, you can connect it to your other business tools using Zapier or Make. When a new lead fills in your form, the agent drafts the proposal automatically. When a client submits a brief, the agent builds the project plan.

07D · Responsible Use

Using Claude Responsibly

AI is a powerful tool. Like any powerful tool, how you use it matters. Here's a clear, honest framework for using Claude in a way that serves your business — and your clients — well.

✓ Do This

Use AI to amplify your expertise

  • Use Claude to draft — then review, edit, and own the output
  • Load your own knowledge and experience as context files
  • Use AI for the repetitive parts so you can focus on the parts that need you
  • Tell clients when AI has assisted in producing work, where relevant
  • Fact-check any statistics, figures, or claims Claude produces
  • Use Claude to think through problems — not to replace your judgment
✗ Avoid This

Don't outsource your judgment

  • Don't send AI-generated content without reading it first
  • Don't share confidential client data with AI tools without checking your data agreements
  • Don't use AI to produce work in someone else's name without their knowledge
  • Don't rely on Claude for legal, medical, or financial advice — it can assist, not advise
  • Don't assume Claude is always right — it can be confidently wrong
  • Don't automate decisions that require human empathy or accountability
⚠ Handle With Care

Grey areas to navigate thoughtfully

  • Client communications: AI-assisted is fine; AI-only without review is risky
  • Personal data: GDPR applies — don't upload identifiable client data to third-party AI tools without a data processing agreement
  • Creative work: AI can assist but the creative direction should be yours
  • Sensitive conversations: redundancy, complaints, difficult feedback — keep humans in the loop
  • Public-facing content: always review before publishing, especially anything factual

The FCO Principle on AI

AI should make you more capable, not less accountable. The goal is not to remove humans from the loop — it's to remove humans from the parts of the loop that don't need them. Your judgment, your relationships, your expertise: these are irreplaceable. Claude handles the volume. You handle the value.

🔒
On data privacy: Claude Pro and Claude for Work both have options to opt out of training data usage. For client work, use Claude for Work (Teams or Enterprise plan) which includes a data processing agreement and guarantees your conversations are not used to train models. Check Anthropic's current privacy policy at anthropic.com/privacy before uploading sensitive business data.
